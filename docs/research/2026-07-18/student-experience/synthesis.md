# Student-Experience Research Synthesis (RFC-004 fleet)

- **Status:** Complete
- **Date:** 2026-07-18
- **Inputs:** rq01–rq05 reports in this directory; `docs/research/2026-07-18/synthesis.md` (notes-domain synthesis, for consistency with ADR-0026); `docs/rfc/0003-learny-v3-roadmap.md` (house cycle style and the pending Cycle F).
- **Output:** provisional cycle scoping for RFC-004, respecting the locked decisions (reading-first, Home/Reader/Review IA, bookish identity evolved from shadcn, frontend-led boundary with student-workflow-shaped backend state only, 14-day dogfood gate). The visual-identity *choice* is deliberately left open — this synthesis recommends, the author decides.

## What the fleet found

**rq01 (reading surfaces).** Learny's reader is the outlier in its own survey: one section per click at 14px sans, no position, no progress, while every surveyed product (Readwise Reader, Kindle, Apple Books, Matter, Foliate) renders a continuous flow with persistent position. The core move is a **chapter-flow view** — all sections of a chapter as one scrollable article, block anchors as DOM ids so citations deep-link *within* the flow. Scroll (not pagination) is the desktop-web default everywhere; typography consensus is serif ~19–20px / ~65ch / 1.55–1.6 leading (Literata proposed, OFL, full Portuguese coverage); appearance settings funnel behind a single `Aa` popover; chrome recedes while reading; Kindle's cycled progress metrics (percent / position / min-left-in-chapter) and Foliate's back-after-jump history set the navigation bar. Reading position + per-book progress is exactly the backend state the boundary sanctions.

**rq02 (inline AI interactions).** The consensus invocation stack is: selection in the text triggers AI; answers accumulate in a non-modal side panel; nobody credible uses modals or routes selection questions to another page — Learny's standalone Ask page is the anti-pattern to kill. "Explain" (one tap, selection-as-input) and "Ask" (input + conversation) are distinct verbs everywhere that matters. NotebookLM and Acrobat jointly set the citation bar: numbered markers, hover shows the verbatim passage, click scrolls the source in place and highlights it, locators attached. Learny's `citations.tsx` skeleton is directionally right but shows chunk snippets and does a one-way route change — the fix (anchor-resolved passage in the reading face; click = in-reader scroll-to-anchor while the answer stays in the panel) is the moment the locked "citations resolve to passages" principle becomes literal. No competitor has a tutoring mode; reader-column + Teach-in-panel is open ground. Two cheap wins: suggested prompts in the empty panel, and save-answer-to-anchored-note (rides ADR-0026).

**rq03 (highlight → note → card pipeline).** Five ecosystems converge on one pipeline shape with two invariants: anchor inheritance is automatic (the user never re-cites — RemNote's pin, SuperMemo's propagating references; Anki's convention-based source field is the documented decay mode), and every promotion stage is optional and one gesture from where the user is (context switches kill the pipeline). RemNote is the reference implementation: highlight → linked note in one paste, AI-suggested cards accepted one click at a time, review cards carrying a pin that opens the original sentence. Learny's back half (layered anchors + reconcile, notes/note_anchors, quiz pipeline with QC gate, FSRS) is largely in place; the Reader must add the front half: the capture popover as the *only* capture surface, AI card suggestion at the highlight via the existing pipeline (accept/edit/discard chips, never silent bulk), a margin rail with orphan surfacing, review pins, and single-key shortcuts. Explicit exclusions: inline card syntax in notes, standalone card-authoring pages, silent auto-generation, SuperMemo priority-queue mechanics. One correction against the repo: rq03 describes note→quiz and the Obsidian export as "already built (Cycles E–F)" — git history shows only RFC-003 Cycle E merged (PR #31); the note→quiz and export *designs* are locked by ADR-0026 but Cycle F is still unbuilt. The sequencing below accounts for this.

**rq04 (Home & re-entry ritual).** Duolingo's most-validated finding is to decouple the habit metric from the volume metric — showing up counts, volume is displayed but never demanded — and grace beats rigidity (freezes/smoothing *increased* retention). The calm form of gamification is proven safe (Anki users mass-install a heatmap; LingQ users beg to disable streak popups). Every successful re-entry screen is one tap to the next action, with bounded daily work ("finished for now"). Proposed Home: a continue-reading hero (Readwise's >5%-progress honesty rule), a due-cards card with a "Done for today" state, a below-the-fold heatmap + adherence-framed streak ("Studied 12 of the last 14 days" — the same number as the success gate), and nothing else. Backend cost: two small tables (`reading_position`, `study_days` rollup), streak computed at read time, never stored; no notifications, email, or provider changes.

**rq05 (design identity).** The current base is stock shadcn neutral with Geist everywhere including book prose — the real gap is that the reading surface is typographically indistinguishable from the settings page. Because every audited component styles via semantic tokens, three of four directions are ~a `globals.css` rewrite + one font binding + a reader prose class. Four fully specified directions: **A "Marginalia"** (warm, Literata, library green, running-head signature), **B "Iron Gall"** (cool/inky, Source Serif 4, Prussian blue, ink-line rule doubling as reading progress), **C "Tipografia"** (Iberian, Alegreya superfamily incl. UI-font swap, oxblood + gold, drop caps — highest cost), **D "Typescript"** (graphite + azure, Newsreader + iA Writer Quattro two-voice signature). rq05 recommends B (furthest from the AI-default cream/terracotta cluster, keeps Geist, strongest dark mode for night study, functional signature), stealing warm marker highlights and D's streaming caret; A is runner-up if more warmth is wanted. Highlight colors must be direction-independent `--highlight-*` tokens kept warm even on cool palettes. All faces are OFL/free and self-hostable.

## Tensions and resolutions

1. **How many verbs on the selection popover?** rq01 says four modes, rq02 says five (Highlight · Note · Explain · Ask · Create card), rq03 says three escalations (Note / Make cards / Ask-Teach). **Resolution: adopt rq02's five-verb enumeration** — the survey evidence that Explain and Ask are distinct verbs is strong and Explain is nearly free (fixed prompt over the selection). Teach enters from the panel and from an overflow on the popover rather than a sixth top-level verb; rq03's three "escalations" are the same set minus Explain, so there is no real conflict, only granularity.
2. **Auto-highlight on selection?** rq03 documents Readwise's zero-action auto-highlight as best-in-class; rq01 and Learny's shipped CapturePopover use select → deliberate gesture. **Resolution: deliberate single action by default, auto-highlight as a toggle default-off** — rq03 itself recommends this for a books-not-articles reading style, and it avoids accidental-highlight noise in long study sessions. The enforced friction budget stands: highlight ≤1 action, note = 1 keystroke + typing, AI card = 2 actions, jump-back = 1 click.
3. **Identity-first or reader-first?** rq05 wants three styling PRs starting with tokens+fonts; rq01 wants the chapter-flow rebuild as the core move. Building the reader unstyled means restyling it later; blocking the reader on a taste decision inverts priorities. **Resolution: run the identity *prototype gate + tokens/fonts* as a small first cycle** (the serif-on-reader change rq05 says should land "regardless of which direction wins"), so the reader rebuild lands styled from day one; the direction's *signature system* and the annotation/citation restyle land later, with the surfaces they decorate (per rq05's own PR sequencing).
4. **Where does the signature element live?** B's ink-line doubles as reading progress (a Cycle-B reader concern); A's running head is reader chrome; D's caret is an Ask-panel concern. **Resolution: the chosen direction's signature ships with the cycle that builds its host surface** (reader chrome → reader cycle; streaming caret → panel cycle), not as a standalone styling pass — this keeps every cycle independently shippable and avoids decorating surfaces twice.
5. **Streaks vs the calm identity.** rq04 imports Duolingo evidence into a product whose identity forbids Duolingo's register. **Resolution: adopt the mechanics, reject the register** — showing-up streak with silent grace and adherence framing, heatmap below the fold, a "hide stats" setting, and a hard code-level cap: no XP/coins/badges/popups/notifications anywhere in v3. rq04 already resolves this internally; the synthesis elevates it to an RFC-level constraint.
6. **rq03's "already built" vs the repo.** rq03 treats RFC-003 Cycle F machinery (note→quiz, export) as shipped; only Cycle E is merged. **Resolution: RFC-004's capture cycles build only against what Cycle E shipped (highlights, notes, anchors, reconcile) plus the v2 quiz pipeline; RFC-003 Cycle F is re-sequenced to run *after* the reader surfaces it needs exist** (see below). "Make cards" at the highlight uses the existing v2 quiz pipeline scoped to the highlighted quote — it does not depend on Cycle F's note→quiz path.
7. **Spoiler-scoped retrieval and the daily email.** Both rq02 and rq04 flag genuinely good ideas (Kindle position-scoped answers; Readwise's email ritual) that cross the frozen boundary. **Resolution: defer both, but design for them** — ordered anchors + `reading_position` make position-scoping cheap later; the Review card's content shape should be projectable into a digest without new domain state. Neither enters any RFC-004 cycle.
8. **Consistency with the notes-domain synthesis.** The earlier synthesis deferred "highlight↔note cardinality and margin-display UX" to design. This fleet answers it: notes captured from the Reader are margin notes on the shipped notes domain (a `notes` row + minted `note_anchors` row), surfaced in a margin rail with orphan quote snapshots — no new object, no schema change. ADR-0026's invariants (minted IDs, FSRS never implicitly reset, drift badge with explicit reset) are load-bearing in the capture cycles and must not be re-opened.

## Provisional RFC-004 cycle scoping

Ordering rationale (mirroring RFC-003's style): the smallest taste-gated decision first so everything after it lands styled; the reader rebuild next because every other surface hangs off it; AI-in-reader before capture-pipeline because it deletes the standalone pages the locked IA removes and ships the citation component that RFC-003 Cycle F needs; Home late because it consumes state the reader cycle creates; polish + dogfood last. Sizes: S ≈ a few tasks, M ≈ a normal ship-cycle, L ≈ the largest single cycle in the RFC.

### Cycle A — Identity gate: direction choice, tokens, reading typography (S)

- Side-by-side prototype route rendering one real Portuguese-language chapter and one citation popover under the candidate token sets (rq05's prototype gate); **the author picks the direction** — options are A "Marginalia", B "Iron Gall", C "Tipografia", D "Typescript" as specified in rq05. This synthesis endorses rq05's recommendation (**B**, runner-up **A**, steal B's warm markers + D's streaming caret) but the choice is explicitly the author's and stays open until this gate.
- Ship the direction-independent floor immediately after the pick: `globals.css` token rewrite (light + dark, WCAG-verified pairs), `--font-serif` binding (self-hosted via `next/font`), `--highlight-*` warm marker tokens, `.prose-reading` class applied to the existing reader and citation popovers, micro-typography discipline in corpus Markdown rendering.
- Codify the winner in a short ADR (palette, type roles, token names, signature rules) so later cycles and AI-generated components inherit it.
- Depends on: nothing. Unblocks: styled surfaces in every later cycle.

### Cycle B — Reader core: chapter flow, position, progress (L)

- Chapter-flow view: all sections of the current chapter as one continuous scrollable article; every block/heading keeps its stable anchor as a DOM `id`; `?anchor=` scrolls within the flow with the existing transient highlight; sticky prev/next-chapter at the boundaries; scroll, not pagination.
- Reading typography defaults live here (19px serif, ~65ch, 1.6 leading, ragged-right, `lang` from book language, `hyphens: auto` behind a justify toggle); single `Aa` popover (size, spacing, light/sepia/dark theme on the two-axis Apple Books model); receding top bar; TOC sidebar with position context, read-state ticks, and back-to-previous-position after any jump; load-path fix (no auth→section waterfall, text-shaped skeleton).
- Backend (sanctioned state): `reading_position` per (user, source) — corpus anchor + updated_at + denormalized percent — written on scroll-idle; per-chapter position and "~N min left in chapter" from corpus word counts. Existing highlights render inline as anchored spans.
- The chosen direction's chrome signature (e.g., B's ink-line-as-progress or A's running head) ships here, since this cycle builds its host surface.
- Depends on: Cycle A (tokens/fonts). This is the center of gravity of the RFC — the reading-first principle made literal.

### Cycle C — Ask & Teach in the reader: panel, verbs, citations-as-passages (M) — **unblocks RFC-003 Cycle F**

- Kill the standalone Ask and Teach pages; port `ask-screen.tsx`'s transport/streaming into a non-modal Reader side panel; old routes redirect into the reader. Suggested prompts in the empty panel state; the chosen direction's streaming caret (if adopted) lands here.
- Selection popover grows to the five verbs — Highlight · Note · Explain · Ask · Create card — all anchor-inheriting (Create card wires fully in Cycle D; here it may stub to the note path or ship thin). Explain = one-tap fixed prompt over the selection; Ask = panel input scoped to the selection.
- Citations upgrade: popover renders the verbatim anchor-resolved passage in the reading serif with section-path locators (expanding to the enclosing block on ragged chunk edges — the reader never sees chunk machinery); click = in-reader scroll-to-anchor with transient highlight while the answer stays in the panel; references list for many-citation answers.
- Teach becomes a mode of the same panel (entry from popover overflow + panel control), keeping the taught passage visible; teaching-turn citations reuse the passage-link component. "Save answer to anchored note" on Ask/Teach responses (rides ADR-0026).
- Depends on: Cycle B (the flow gives citations their scroll targets). **This is the cycle that unblocks RFC-003 Cycle F**: notes-join-retrieval needs the panel + citation-passage component to present "your note" citations distinctly, and note→quiz provenance reuses the same anchor-resolution surface. Schedule RFC-003 Cycle F after this cycle (ideally after Cycle D, which adds the review pin that Cycle F's "provenance shown at review" rides on).

### Cycle D — Capture pipeline: cards at the highlight, margin rail, review pins (M)

- "Create card" completes: the existing v2 quiz-generation pipeline scoped to the highlighted quote + chunk, rendered as accept/edit/discard suggestion chips (RemNote AI Cards pattern); accepted cards minted with stable IDs and typed provenance per ADR-0026; no silent bulk generation, no new generation code paths.
- Margin rail: per-section highlights + anchored notes, orphans included with quote snapshots and resurrection affordances — the orphan-surfacing UI the anchor cascade requires, and the reverse direction (reading → my past thinking).
- Review pins: every card face shows typed provenance ("*Book*, §3.2" / "your note *Title*") and one click opens the Reader at the anchor (or note detail), drift badge alongside — a card without a working pin is a broken citation.
- Single-key vocabulary across capture and review (`H`/`N`/`C`, grade keys, `O` open source) with a `?` overlay. Enforced friction budget from rq03. Explicit exclusions carried into the RFC: inline card syntax, standalone card-authoring page, silent auto-generation, priority-queue/incremental-reading mechanics.
- Depends on: Cycle B (rail + pins target the flow); shares the popover with Cycle C. Completes the unblock of RFC-003 Cycle F's review-provenance half.

### Cycle E — Home, Review re-entry, and the IA rewire (M)

- Home as a quiet two-card study desk: continue-reading hero (cover, chapter/section, human-readable position, one click → reader at the exact anchor; >5%-progress honesty rule) + due-cards card (count + est. minutes, one button into the FSRS queue, "Done for today" completion state) + below-the-fold days-studied heatmap with adherence-framed streak ("Studied X of the last 14 days" — the same number as the success gate). Nothing else on the page; "hide reading stats" setting.
- Backend (sanctioned state): `study_days` (user, date) rollup upserted by existing write paths (reading-position writes, review answers, note/card creation); streak and adherence computed at read time, never stored. Any study event counts — showing up, not volume.
- IA rewire completes: Library demoted to a bookshelf (covers + per-book progress from `reading_position`), standalone Ask/Teach routes removed (redirects from Cycle C confirmed), top-level nav = Home / Reader / Review.
- Hard gamification cap in code: no XP, coins, badges, leaderboards, celebration modals, or notifications anywhere. Deferred with design hooks: daily email digest, position-scoped retrieval.
- Depends on: Cycle B for `reading_position` (hard); Cycle C for route removal (soft — can land behind it). May be pulled earlier in parallel with C/D if the dogfood ritual wants an earlier start.

### Cycle F — Polish, signature completion, and the dogfood gate (S–M)

- Remaining identity work: annotation/citation restyle per the chosen direction (margin markers, chip/popover treatment), any signature elements not yet landed, WCAG re-verification across final token pairings, empty/loading/error states swept to the identity.
- Start the 14-day dogfood clock: the author studies daily in Learny; defects found during the window are fixed in-place (small PRs, no new scope). The gate is the RFC's success criterion — Home's "X of 14" strip is its instrument.
- RFC-004 retrospective; feeds the v3 wrap (README/demo refresh, retrospective, 0.3.0) that RFC-003 Cycle F carries.
- Depends on: everything above.

### Sequencing note — RFC-003 Cycle F

RFC-003's Cycle F (notes join retrieval with distinct "your note" citations; note→quiz through the pipeline with provenance at review; Obsidian export; 0.3.0 wrap) remains unbuilt and is intentionally interleaved: run it **after RFC-004 Cycle C** (its citation presentation and panel exist) and preferably after **Cycle D** (its review provenance rides the pin). Its backend halves (notes retrieval arms, export projection) have no RFC-004 dependency and could start earlier in parallel, but shipping its user-facing surfaces against the old reader would be immediate rework — the reason RFC-004 precedes it at all.
