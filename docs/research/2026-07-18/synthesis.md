- **Cross-report tension #1 (resolved below)**: rq03 models notes as whole Markdown documents with derived indexes rebuilt on save, while rq06's quiz identity wants stable per-block IDs across edits. Rebuilt-on-save derived blocks do not automatically preserve minted IDs — the ADR resolves this by making block-ID persistence a reconcile-on-save match (same philosophy as anchor reconcile), and by anchoring quiz identity to the minted ID, never the content hash.
- **Cross-report convergence**: rq01/rq02/rq03/rq06/rq07 all independently land on the shipped quiz-item pattern (value snapshots, no corpus FK, reconcile-on-re-ingest, projection exports) as the house mechanism — strong signal the notes domain composes with what exists rather than needing new machinery.

- **Biggest risk in the recommended direction**: the rq02 anchor cascade is the deepest new mechanism and lands in Cycle E. Mitigation adopted: ship exact tiers only (anchor/alias → block-hash → quote search), persist orphans with telemetry, defer fuzzy matching until orphan evidence exists — rq02 itself recommends this bound.
- **Re-check before Cycle F ships**: NotebookLM's flashcard/quiz trajectory (rq01 open issue) — if Google adds scheduling, the positioning section of ADR-0026 needs a refresh, not the architecture.
